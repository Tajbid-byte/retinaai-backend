Methodology

Three-Phase Research Pipeline

A rigorous end-to-end pipeline from raw fundus image collection through explainable ensemble classification.

01
Data Preparation
▾
Data Acquisition
APTOS 2019 fundus images from Vision-Star cameras across multiple Indian ophthalmology clinics.
EDA & Visualisation
Class distribution analysis, image quality profiling, and feature visualization before modeling.
Class Imbalance Handling
SMOTE augmentation and class-weighted loss to address severe inter-grade imbalance.
Preprocessing
Ben Graham preprocessing, CLAHE contrast enhancement, Gaussian blur and normalization to 224×224.
02
Model Training Pipeline
▾
K-Fold Cross Validation
2-Fold, 4-Fold, and 6-Fold configurations to rigorously evaluate generalization performance.
Transfer Learning
8 ImageNet-pretrained CNN architectures fine-tuned with frozen base layers and custom classification heads.
Feature Extraction
GlobalAveragePooling2D compresses spatial feature maps into compact 1×1×F representations.
Head Training
Dense(1024, ReLU) → Dropout(0.5) → Dense(5, Softmax) custom classification head per backbone.
03
Finalization & XAI Analysis
▾
Model Evaluation
Accuracy, Precision, F1-Score, Recall and Confusion Matrix across all models and folds.
Top-N Selection
Best-performing models ranked by weighted F1-score; top candidates selected for stacking ensemble.
CNN–CatBoost Stacking
Softmax probability meta-features fed into CatBoost gradient boosting meta-learner.
LIME XAI
Superpixel-level explanation maps for individual predictions enabling clinical transparency.

Explainability

XAI Analysis with LIME

The core research contribution — high accuracy without sacrificing clinical explainability through Local Interpretable Model-agnostic Explanations.

🔍
LIME Superpixel Maps
LIME generates pixel-level importance maps highlighting which retinal regions most strongly influenced the model's prediction — enabling clinicians to validate AI reasoning against known pathology locations.
🧠
Pathology Localization
For Severe NPDR (Grade 3) cases, LIME correctly localizes microaneurysms, flame hemorrhages, and venous beading — the exact features ophthalmologists use for clinical grading.
⚖️
Accuracy–Explainability Balance
CNN–CatBoost stacking preserves per-model interpretability. CatBoost feature importance over the probability meta-features further quantifies each CNN's contribution to the final decision.
🏥
Clinical Translation
LIME explanations provide ophthalmologists with trustworthy visual rationale — supporting informed decision-making and regulatory pathways for AI-assisted DR screening deployment.
